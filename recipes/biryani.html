Biryani


Description

Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic South Asian rice dish made by layering basmati rice with marinated meat (such as chicken, mutton, or beef), yogurt, fried onions, and a blend of spices like cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and saffron. The meat is often marinated in yogurt, garlic, ginger, and spices, then cooked with partially boiled rice. The layers are steamed together (a method called dum) to allow the flavors to meld. Garnished with fresh herbs like cilantro and mint, biryani is typically served with raita or salad.

Ingredients

  • Basmati rice – 2 cups
  • Chicken (bone-in) – 500g
  • Yogurt – 1 cup
  • Onions (thinly sliced) – 2 large
  • Tomatoes – 2 medium
  • Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tbsp
  • Green chilies – 2
  • Red chili powder – 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder – ½ tsp
  • Garam masala – 1 tsp
  • Coriander powder – 1 tsp
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom)
  • Fresh coriander leaves – a handful
  • Fresh mint leaves – a handful
  • Saffron – a pinch (optional)
  • Milk – 2 tbsp (to soak saffron)
  • Ghee or oil – 3 tbsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Water – as needed

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt, spices, and ginger-garlic paste (30 mins or more).
  2. Fry onions until golden brown; set some aside for garnish.
  3. Cook marinated chicken with tomatoes and fried onions until tender.
  4. Boil basmati rice with whole spices until 70% cooked; drain.
  5. Layer rice and chicken in a pot, topping with mint, coriander, saffron milk, and fried onions.
  6. Cover and steam (dum cook) on low heat for 20–25 minutes.
  7. Fluff and serve with raita or salad.
